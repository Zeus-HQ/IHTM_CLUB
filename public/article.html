    Students Spotlight:

    Shefaa Alferaidi, aged 28 from Medina, grew up in a family that encouraged her to be responsible and well educated. She got accepted into Taibah University, and was thrown into the Chemistry Dpt., despite it not being any of her preferred majors. It was an endless road with no way out but forward, so she settled for the curve ball that was thrown at her and received a Bachelor’s degree in Chemistry. Unfortunately, there was a rarity in jobs related to her major, and thus she was unable find a job. With no other choice, she was forced to stay at home. But it was time spent wisely, as Shefaa did not waste a single second of it. She took tons of courses and started her online business providing purchase services from international sites.

    Sometime after that, an opportunity arose to work as an admin in a private school, and one she did not let pass. Whilst working, she joined the Education Evaluation Authority as a supervisor of national tests for a short while. As fate would have it, after two years of working at the school, Shefaa would come to receive a promotion as manager of the school. And as manager, she was able to organize many events alongside the authority to hire proficient teachers, all the while receiving extra income. For the time she worked at the school, Shefaa thoroughly enjoyed her job. But it was unfortunate that the owner of the school was more interested in the material aspect of things and not the development of his employees and faculty A year later when it all seemed to come to an end, Shefaa tried to hang on as much as she was able , before resigning in order to pay dues to her employees, who numbered more than twenty. Yet that wasn't the end of her journey, she participated in Wateen blood donation campaigns and became commissioned in Medina at that time. As time went on, she grabbed on to every passing opportunity to improve and develop her skills. Shefaa had the chance to be one of the representatives of The Chamber of Commerce at the Medina Shopping Festival. Soon after, she got accepted in the scholarship of the Red Sea Development company.

    "There is always a chance for every diligent person, and every door closes, God opens thousand and behind it"

    Coral Bloom

    Shurayrah is the hub island of The Red Sea Project on the west coast of Saudi Arabia. With a natural dolphin shape, the island is one of 92 in our archipelago, which is home to the world's fourth largest barrier reef system, untouched corals and a significant number of endangered species. We appointed renowned British architects Foster + Partners to design all 11 unique resorts on Shurayrah. Referred to as "Coral Bloom", the designs are sure to give future guests a barefoot luxury experience like no other place on earth. The Shurayrah resorts make up the majority of the 16 hotels in phase one of the Project, due to open in 2023. From a remarkable vision to a groundbreaking reality, work is well underway on Shurayrah Island as the world’s most ambitious regenerative tourism development takes shape.

    Story of the Issue:

    Nawaf Al Mutairi talks about his journey in the Hospitality & Tourism industry Mr. Al Mutairi began his career in May 2015, despite the fact that he was studying Mechanical Engineering at that time, it did not stop him from standing out among his colleagues in Anwar Al Medina Movenpick Hotel. He admitted that he faced many struggles at first, but as soon as he was able to reconcile between his bachelor major and his career in hospitality, there were no further obstacles in his path to success. Mr. Autairi has stated that time management is what enabled him to launch in with confidence. He started his journey as a receptionist and climbed the ladder of success until he was promoted to duty manager in 2018. Between those two positions, he was able to gain experience in almost everything in relation the ‘front-of-house’ department. He also that he aspires to take on more, due to the massive evolution of tourism in Saudi Arabia. He expects a bright future for Saudi Arabia in the Hospitality Industry with the launching all the major projects, such as The Red Sea Project, Neom and Al Qiddiya. Within the next 3 to 5 years, Saudi Arabia will not be limited to religious tourism, as it will attract more local and international tourists. On a personal level and from his own experience, he has stated that he felt delighted to be working with Saudi partners and colleagues, and as he referred to them ad "the national wealth". He describes males as hard workers and mentioned that adding the female component enhanced the value of the industry, especially in customers service. In addition, Mr. Al Mutairi said that patience and self-control are key in the field, and will be helpful with clients and employees, alongside body language and communication skills, especially with co-workers. He said: “Good relations between colleagues will facilitate the work between departments.” Regarding the work environment, Mr. Al Mutairi gave us some golden advice from his personal experience, “As future stars in the Hospitality Industry, you must be prepared at all times, to face work stress and expect the worst, which will increase your promptitude and your ability to handle all matters”. For instance, if you have a birthday in one room and a death case in another one, you must split between both of them by giving each guest the kind of care that they need. You have to train yourself to acquire the skill of poker face. Mr. Al Mutairi has emphasized on the importance of combining work experience with the academic knowledge, which will always make you a better candidate.
